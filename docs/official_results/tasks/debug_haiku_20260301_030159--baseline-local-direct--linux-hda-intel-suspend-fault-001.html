I've completed my fault localization analysis of the HDA Intel sound driver issue. Here's a summary of my findings:

## Analysis Summary

**Bug**: HDA Intel sound stops working after suspend to RAM (regression in v3.7.1 from v3.6.11)

**Root Cause**: A race condition in the `azx_init_chip()` function in `sound/pci/hda/hda_intel.c`

### The Problem

The initialization sequence in `azx_init_chip()` has a critical ordering issue:

1. **Line 1219**: `azx_int_enable(chip)` - Enables hardware interrupts
2. **Line 1223**: `azx_init_cmd_io(chip)` - Initializes CORB/RIRB (codec command/response buffers)

**The race condition**: After `azx_reset()`, the hardware may generate interrupts immediately, especially after resume from suspend. If interrupts arrive before the RIRB is initialized (between lines 1219-1223), the interrupt handler `azx_interrupt()` will call `azx_update_rirb()` to process codec responses, but:

- The RIRB read/write pointers haven't been reset yet (line 751 in `azx_init_cmd_io`)
- The RIRB data structures haven't been memset (line 752)
- This causes the RIRB state to become corrupted

When codec communication resumes via `snd_hda_resume()`, commands timeout because the RIRB pointers are out of sync.

### The Fix

Move `azx_init_cmd_io(chip)` **before** `azx_int_enable(chip)` to ensure the RIRB is fully initialized before interrupts are enabled.
